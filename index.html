Category One: Software Engineering and Design

The artifact chosen for category one, Software Engineering and Design, is IT-145 Foundation in App Development. Pet Boarding and Grooming (Pet BAG) is developing software tailored to manage operations for dogs. The goal is to replace the outdated paper-based processes with efficient digital solutions. The system now handles essential functions such as dog check-in and check-out, pet management tasks, feeding schedules and yard times, and administrative features, including updating pet information and tracking payment statuses. It includes specialized capabilities like managing specialized services, available exclusively for pets staying two days or more. The software streamlines operations within the boarding facility, ensuring smooth processes from arrival to departure for all pet guests.

Enhancements to The Dogs Cats Hotel

  • Redesigned System Architecture: Transitioned from a monolithic structure to a modular design.
  • Use of Design Patterns: Applied MVC and Singleton for better resource management.
  • Improved Maintainability: Added documentation and streamlined code formatting.
  • Error Handling: Implemented robust error handling to reduce failures.

Category Two: Algorithms and Data Structures

The artifact chosen for category two is IT-145 Foundation in App Development. The Java-based Pet Hotel Management program employed algorithms to optimize pet care operations. Binary search algorithms ensured quick access to pet records during check-in and check-out. Sorting algorithms facilitated smoother scheduling and space allocation. These implementations enhanced efficiency, reduced processing times, and ensured seamless management.

Category Three: Databases

The chosen artifact for category three focuses on database integration. Within the Java-based Pet Hotel Management program, databases were pivotal for storing pet records such as type, name, and stay duration. Database integration ensured consistency, scalability, and optimized data retrieval tasks.
